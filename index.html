Seven skills

Everything you need, from first profile to progress report.

Set up
/init-learning
Set up your profile

Define who you are, your strengths and gaps, and how you like to learn — one friendly question at a time. Paste in review feedback and it extracts the themes.

Triggers on
"set up learning" · "update my profile"
/new-learning
Find what's next

Turn a gap into 3–5 concrete project or reading ideas — each sized to your session length and broken into milestones — then add the ones you pick to your backlog.

Triggers on
"what should I learn" · "I want to learn X"
Learn
/learn
Run a session

The core experience. Start, resume, or pick up a lesson. Content adapts to your style — guided, challenge-based, or reading — and Claude silently tracks your wins and struggles as you go.

Triggers on
"let's learn" · "pick up where I left off"
/end-learn
Debrief & save

Wrap up with a debrief you review and edit — what you worked on, what clicked, what's still fuzzy. Then it logs the session, updates lesson state, and feeds the intelligence layer.

Triggers on
"I'm done for today" · "save progress"
Review
/learning-status
Quick check-in

A fast, read-only snapshot — current lesson, last session, backlog size, weekly streak, gaps addressed. No generation, instant answer before you decide to dive in.

Triggers on
"where am I" · "check my progress"
/learning-report
See your growth

Aggregate logs and lessons over any timeframe into a real progress report — key learnings, recurring struggles, gap progress, and suggested next steps. Perfect before a 1:1.

Triggers on
"what have I learned" · "learning report"
The intelligence layer

It remembers, so every session gets smarter.

Behind the slash commands runs learning-insights — an internal layer that mines your past sessions. It surfaces topics you found fuzzy, spots struggles that recur across different lessons, and tracks whether each gap is trending toward mastery.

  • ▹ Flags fuzzy topics from past sessions before you start a new one.
  • ▹ Detects patterns that recur across unrelated lessons.
  • ▹ Adjusts difficulty per gap — beginner, intermediate, advanced.
